What is Social Media Management?

Social media management is the process of creating, publishing, promoting, and managing content across social media channels like Facebook, Instagram, Twitter, LinkedIn, Pinterest & YouTube.

Social media management goes beyond just posting updates to your company’s social media profiles. It also includes engaging with your audience and looking for new opportunities to increase reach and visibility.

Choosing the Right Social Media Management Platforms

Choosing the Right Social Media Management Platforms Before you can begin putting together an action plan for social media management, you will need to consider what the best social media platforms are for your business.

Here’s a brief overview of the different platforms and what benefits they can provide your business:

Facebook

With well over a billion monthly active users, Facebook is the largest and most powerful social media advertising platform in the world.

Instagram

A picture says a thousand words. Use one of the most engaging social media platforms that over 60% of people use to discover new products.

Twitter

Increase brand recognition through Twitter. With over 330 million users and is used by all ages, from 18-64. Build relationships through a trusted platform.


LinkedIn

Reach up to 500 million decision-makers and professionals through the most popular social media platform for business owners.


YouTube

YouTube has become the second largest search engine in the world, second only to Google. You can upload your video content to this channel to help engage and connect with your audience.

Pinterest

Pinterest is another great channel for sharing image-focused content. This has become a must for consumers who are interested in DIY, crafts, home improvement, nutrition, fitness, fashion, and more.

Creating and Posting Content to Social Profiles

A big part of social media management is creating and publishing social media content across your company’s profiles. This involves not only writing copy but also developing creative in the form of images or video.

This an aspect of social media management requires you to have an in-depth understanding of social media platforms and your audience. You need to know what the best practices are for creating content on the channel.

Content Designed for Optimal Impact

If you want to keep customers engaged and grow your following on these channels, you’ll need to consistently post quality content. The only way to do this is to create a social media schedule that details when and what you’re going to post on which channels.
